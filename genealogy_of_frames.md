## 2. Why materials testing carries

The discarded frame attempts are load-bearing in suitable application domains; what they did not carry for the claim presented here is the *operative measurement bridge* between abstract self-reference and a concrete measured quantity. Mutual information measures information, not deformation; a thermodynamics-of-mind speaks of entropy, not of load.

Materials testing carries because it holds the operative bridge explicitly: a specimen under load yields, deforms, breaks. The same sequence — load uptake, deformation, inflection, consolidation — is codified in DIN standards, operationalized with a single inflection-point parameter ($\hat{a}$), and transferable to other substrates without sliding into metaphor.

## 3. External convergence as the methodologically stronger evidence for the frame

Independent external convergence points support the frame's load-bearing capacity more strongly than the internal genealogy:

- **Olmeda, Lohoff, Kafetzopoulos et al. (2026)** — *Scaling and self-similarity in the formation of the embryonic epigenome* (Nature Physics, DOI 10.1038/s41567-026-03263-x). Independently finds self-similarity with power-law exponent 5/2 and a real-space geometrical renormalization-group scheme — structurally parallel to the renormalization operator $M = N \circ R \circ C$.
- **Simon, Kunin, Atanasov et al. (2026)** — *There Will Be a Scientific Theory of Deep Learning* (arXiv:2604.21691). Posits a "mechanics of learning" as a discipline in direct parallel to the materials testing of language presented here.
- **Vipulanandan, Premaratne, Sarkar (2026)** — *Semantic Uncertainty Quantification of Hallucinations in LLMs: A Quantum Tensor Network Based Method* (ICLR 2026, arXiv:2601.20026). Uses perturbation theory on a QTN Hamiltonian as a measurement apparatus — a formal-structural parallel to the Birkhoff-Hopf contraction (§3.1, main paper).
- **Chen, Du, Li et al. (2026)** — *The Molecular Structure of Thought: Mapping the Topology of Long Chain-of-Thought Reasoning* (arXiv:2601.06002). Operationalizes long-CoT trajectories as stable molecule-like structures with entropy convergence as the inflection signature.

When researchers in Nature Physics, deep-learning theory, and ICLR **independently** arrive at the same pattern, the frame's load-bearing capacity is structurally grounded, not historical.
